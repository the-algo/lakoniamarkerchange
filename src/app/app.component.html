2016 K-1 Tax Effect - Explore the Potential Tax Effect of investing in an oil drilling partnership

Tax Effect

This Tool helps you do two very important things when considering investing in an oil drilling partnership:

  1. Estimate the impact of an oil drilling partnership on your Estimated After-Tax Income.
  2. Figure out your optimal Investment Amount for each filing situation, as well as estimate the number of Actual Dollars at Risk.

All you need to do to check out the effect is input:

  1. Your Estimated Taxable Income, and
  2. Your Tax Filing Status

By providing two pieces of information, you will see a side-by-side comparison that the effects could have on you with or without a K-1.

If you have never invested before, you might be wondering what a K-1 is. A Schedule K-1, or K-1, is a tax document that reports all incomes, deductions, credits and losses associated with your share of any partnership in which you are participating. Under SEC Regulation D Rule 506(c), if you participate in a partnership, then the Issuer will provide you with a K-1 for your tax filing purposes.

IMPORTANT: This is not tax advice, but merely an interactive tool showing you the potential tax benefits associated with investing in an oil drilling partnership
